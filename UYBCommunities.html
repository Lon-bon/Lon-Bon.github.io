Citizen Collaboration Circles

What are Circles?

Within a community, a Circle is formed when a trained Circle Facilitator assembles a group of citizens with differing viewpoints to take part in the Unform Your Bias Experience. Citizens agree to complete a 10-minute on-line survey and meet every other week for 3 months. During these 1-1.5 hour meetings, citizens will learn to Uncover, Unpack, and Understand their biases, in order to Unform them.

Circles Continued

Many Citizen Collaboration Circles will want to remain together, as a group to tackle local, regional, national and even global problems. The group will work to develop solutions together and determine how to take action. The range of topics for solutions may include:

  • Environment (large or small scale)
  • Community Programs (Sports, D&I, Education, etc.)
  • Faith-based Integration
  • Local or Regional Infrastructure (Roads, Legal, etc.)
